Here I sit, currently in a strange position i'm not used to.  Last week I quit my job, this will be the first time since college I have not been working full time.  Although, I know I shouldn't really be nervous about it, I can't help it.  I am hoping my time off from work will be as short as possible.  The good news is that in the coming months I can really get myself set and coding full time.  The goal will be a minimum of 40/hrs a week, although I would prefer to closer to 50.  

I have been looking actively and speaking with people regarding bootcamps in the Boston area.  I still have yet to make up my mind, but I feel this approach will be the best path forward for me.  I like having that "outside" pressure that helps push things forward. The couple of camps I am considering are [Launch academy](http://www.launchacademy.com/) and [Metis](http://www.thisismetis.com).  I am also considering the online program [Bloc](http://www.bloc.io).  I know there are huge differences between all these programs and I am working on evaluating what will work best for my learning style and family. 

I am at the point where I have built a few rails apps, and done a ton (relatively speaking) of straight ruby programming.  I really need to work on getting some portfolio projects together and could use some help with my brewing app (more on this later).  I do like the idea of having a mentor who I can bounce questions off of.  Currently a lot of the questions I have I know would be much easier answered via a real person vs. scouring stack overflow and google.  

Ultimately, I like to think that what matters in the end is what you know and can produce.  If I end up doing the bloc program, i'll probably enroll here in the next week or so. Excited to be moving forward!
